# Simulated multi-agent protocol review

Date: 2026-07-11

## Scope and limitation

Five independent role-based agents reviewed the protocol, evidence and publication surfaces from research-methods, reimbursement-domain, reproducibility, governance and adversarial-claims perspectives. Four completed substantive reviews; the governance agent encountered a tooling failure, so governance conclusions are limited to consensus findings independently raised by the other reviewers.

This exercise is an engineering review aid. It is not human participant research review, ethics approval, licence approval, institutional sign-off, peer review or OSF registration approval. It cannot satisfy the repository's human-review gates.

## Consensus disposition

All five protocols require major revision before OSF registration or evidence-grade analysis. The repository is reproducible at the software and metadata level, but not yet at the statistical-decision level. Zero protocols are approved for registration, evidence publication or policy inference.

## Blocking findings

1. Estimands, denominators, source vintages, missingness rules, multiplicity, controls and interpretation thresholds were not executable.
2. Mapping needed duplicate blinded review, adjudication, agreement reporting and comparability vetoes.
3. Cross-currency pooled fixture medians and ratios were invalid without harmonisation.
4. Public schedule-amount missingness was incorrectly labelled medicine price opacity.
5. Coverage-policy documents could not support realised-access or causal claims.
6. Fee schedules supported selected amount relativities, not reward, incentives, value or income.
7. Structural completeness was liable to be confused with scientific readiness.
8. Software release readiness needed separation from research, OSF, evidence and policy readiness.

## Changes adopted

- Added fail-closed scientific protocol sections and made OSF readiness require registered status.
- Added protocol-specific statistical decision appendices.
- Suppressed pooled cross-source price and relativity fixture statistics.
- Renamed the medicine metric to `public_schedule_amount_missingness`.
- Preserved zero-denominator proportions as null.
- Narrowed causal and incentive language.
- Split repository release readiness from research publication, OSF registration, evidence release and policy-claim readiness.

## Remaining human gates

- Signed independent methods/statistical review.
- Clinical, reimbursement and terminology-domain review.
- Source-by-source licence and publication-field review.
- Research governance and ethics determination where applicable.
- Frozen reviewed-source bundles, mappings, contracts and analysis manifest.
- OSF registration decision by accountable human investigators.
