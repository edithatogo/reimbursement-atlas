# Canonical context

This project originated from comparing US CMS reimbursement settings with Australian MBS/PBS. The design expanded to a global public billing-schedule atlas with policy analyses and ontology-aware mappings.

## Core framing

Reimbursement schedules are policy instruments. They encode:

- coverage;
- price/payment;
- restrictions;
- patient exposure;
- provider incentives;
- local discretion;
- transparency;
- diffusion and volume control.

## Key methodological warning

A price field is not automatically comparable across jurisdictions. Determine whether it is:

- professional fee;
- facility payment;
- bundled episode tariff;
- drug list price;
- net payer price;
- benefit/subsidy;
- allowed amount;
- payment point;
- patient copayment;
- provider charge;
- negotiated plan amount.

## First-wave comparator systems

Australia, US Medicare/CMS, England/NHS, Ontario/Canada, New Zealand/PHARMAC, Germany, France, Japan, Taiwan, Netherlands and Switzerland.

## Design bias

Use modern tooling, but keep policy validity ahead of technical novelty.
